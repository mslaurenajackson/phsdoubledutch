Biography

LT Lauren Alethea Jackson

Speech-Language Pathologist ¬∑ Therapist Category

LT Lauren Alethea Jackson is a Speech-Language Pathologist in the United States Public Health Service Commissioned Corps with over 16 years of experience serving pediatric populations. A native New Yorker, LT Jackson's commitment to public service is deeply rooted in her family history, inspired by her ancestor, Renty Greaves, who served honorably in Company D, 21st United States Colored Infantry.

LT Jackson specializes in the assessment and treatment of speech sound disorders and holds certification in the PROMPT (Prompts for Restructuring Oral Muscular Phonetic Targets) method. Her clinical expertise is grounded in evidence-based practice and a strong commitment to improving communication outcomes for children and families.

LT Jackson earned her Bachelor of Science in Creative Arts Therapy from C.W. Post Campus of Long Island University and her Master of Science in Communication Sciences and Disorders from Howard University in Washington, D.C. She also holds a bachelor-level certificate in Cybersecurity, reflecting her interest in emerging systems supporting healthcare delivery.

LT Jackson is honored to serve in the USPHS Commissioned Corps and is dedicated to advancing public health through compassionate care, professional excellence, and service to vulnerable communities.
